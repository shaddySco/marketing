About the International Office

In this era of globalization, institutions of higher learning cannot afford to confine themselves only to what is happening within the borders but must also focus on the developments in the international arena with an aim of remaining globally competitive.

The University strives to integrate an international and intercultural dimension into its curriculum, research, and service functions, as these exchanges spur creativity and innovation. Internationalization activities have been aligned with the current DeKUT Strategic Plan 2023-2028. This plan acknowledges the numerous changes impacting the University, such as the growing student and staff population, an expanding research endeavor, evolving learning methods, increasing expectations from students, globalization, environmental awareness, and financial realities.

The creation of an International Office aims to strengthen international activities at the University.

Vision

“To be the leading Center for DeKUT on all matters relating to internationalization of the University.”

Mission

“To provide a resourceful, welcoming and supportive atmosphere for international students and staff, as well as DeKUT students and staff; embracing diversity, cultural sensitivity, and harmonious integration, while promoting the University internationally.”

Objectives of the Office

  • To provide appropriate support services to international students and staff while in residence at DeKUT in liaison with other relevant university sections.
  • To serve as a Resource Centre for international education at DeKUT with particular emphasis on information requirements for both international and DeKUT students, staff, and other stakeholders.
  • To coordinate visits to DeKUT by international scholars in liaison with other university sections.
  • To foster intercultural integration for international students and staff within the community.

Services Provided by the Office

  • Logistical support and facilitation for international visitors, including travel and accommodation arrangements.
  • Assistance with immigration matters such as work permits, visa acquisition, and student passes.
  • Organization and coordination of orientation programs for new international students, staff, and visiting professors.
  • Arranging visits to National Parks and Game Reserves for international students at subsidized rates to enhance their stay, promote Kenyan culture, and market the country.
  • Informing the University of any changing laws and regulations concerning international students and staff.
  • Developing relationships with embassies, other universities, and funding agencies to support the University’s international goals.
  • Providing a welcoming environment to address the unique needs and concerns of international students and staff.
  • Offering guidance, advice, and support to DeKUT students and staff on international matters.
  • Facilitating the exchange of students between DeKUT and international institutions.
