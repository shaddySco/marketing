About Highschool Mentorship

Our Highschool Mentorship Program is designed to help high school students transition smoothly into university life. By providing hands-on experiences, offering career guidance, and exposing students to academic opportunities, we aim to inspire and empower the next generation of leaders. The program bridges the gap between high school and higher education, offering a glimpse into university life and helping students make informed decisions about their future academic and career paths.

Mentorship Methods

1. Campus Visits

Our campus visits are designed to give high school students an authentic experience of university life. The students are guided through key facilities such as lecture halls, libraries, laboratories, and recreational areas. They interact with faculty members and current university students, ask questions about academic programs, and gain valuable insights into student life. These visits help demystify university education and allow students to make informed decisions about their future studies. The campus visit is led by our Marketing Champions, who share their personal journeys and offer career advice to the students.

2. School Outreach

Our Marketing Champions also visit high schools to offer motivation and share their personal experiences in higher education. During outreach events, we engage with students through interactive discussions, workshops, and one-on-one sessions. These activities are tailored to help students explore potential career paths, understand the courses available at our university, and learn how to overcome challenges as they prepare for further education. The outreach program is a powerful way to connect with students directly and provide them with a roadmap for success beyond high school.
